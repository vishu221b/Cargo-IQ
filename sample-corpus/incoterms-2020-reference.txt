INCOTERMS 2020 — QUICK REFERENCE NOTES
(For practitioner use; refer to ICC Publication No. 723E for the canonical text.)

These rules govern the allocation of cost, risk, and responsibility between
seller and buyer in international sales of goods. They do NOT transfer title
to the goods, do NOT cover the contract of sale itself, and do NOT replace the
contract of carriage.

Two groups:

  Group 1 — Rules for any mode or modes of transport (7 rules):
    EXW — Ex Works
    FCA — Free Carrier
    CPT — Carriage Paid To
    CIP — Carriage and Insurance Paid To
    DAP — Delivered at Place
    DPU — Delivered at Place Unloaded
    DDP — Delivered Duty Paid

  Group 2 — Rules for sea and inland waterway transport only (4 rules):
    FAS — Free Alongside Ship
    FOB — Free On Board
    CFR — Cost and Freight
    CIF — Cost, Insurance and Freight

Key 2020 changes from Incoterms 2010:
  - DAT (Delivered At Terminal) renamed to DPU (Delivered at Place Unloaded),
    broadening the scope from terminals to any place where goods can be
    unloaded.
  - CIP now requires Institute Cargo Clauses (A) — all-risks — minimum
    insurance cover. CIF continues to require only ICC (C) minimum, reflecting
    bulk-commodity practice. Buyers under CIF should request an upgrade if
    they need broader cover, and pay the cost difference.
  - FCA now permits the buyer to instruct the carrier to issue an on-board
    bill of lading to the seller, removing a long-standing impedance to using
    FCA (rather than FOB) for containerised cargo.
  - Security-related obligations and the corresponding allocation of costs
    are made explicit in each rule.

Common misuses to avoid:
  - FOB for containerised cargo. Container cargo is handed to the carrier well
    before loading on board; FCA aligns risk transfer with that reality.
  - DDP without confirming the seller can act as importer of record in the
    destination country. In many jurisdictions (incl. Australia) this requires
    a registered local entity.
  - CIF and assuming the insurance is comprehensive. It is the minimum cover,
    deliberately. Specify ICC (A) explicitly if needed and price accordingly.
